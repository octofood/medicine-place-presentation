The Problem

The Black Box: What Happens Today

When an API supplier in India halts production of amoxicillin — Canada's most prescribed antibiotic — this is what follows.

Week 1
Nobody in Ottawa knows. Health Canada has zero visibility into upstream supply chains.
Week 3
Pharmacists notice. Wholesaler backorders appear. A mother in Edmonton drives to three pharmacies. None have children's amoxicillin.
Week 5
Provinces scramble. Emergency imports at 300-400% premiums. Alberta spends $2.1M on product that normally costs $600K.
Week 14
The shortage "resolves." Emergency imports arrive. Prices normalize. Until the next one.
14 weeks from disruption to resolution. Near-zero visibility throughout.
