Guardrails

  • Representative simulated values, not live production telemetry.
  • Illustrative product families, not final commercial SKU commitments.
  • Meant to support Health Canada, PHAC / NESS, PSPC, and resilience readers.
  • Built around oral-first bottlenecks: API, packaging, release, demand shock, and restart time.

What Glass Box Tracks
1. Inventory

Finished goods and API coverage in Canada

This is the clearest near-term availability signal and the most direct answer to “how much room do we have?”

2. Concentration

Single-source dependency and alternate-source status

The framework is explicit: concentration and switch time matter more than vanity supplier counts.

3. Supplier pulse

Delays, quality events, route disruption, and OTIF

This is where earlier warning lives. The signal is upstream before the shortage is visible downstream.

4. Packaging and release

Oral-first bottlenecks that often get ignored

For the Phase 1 oral set, packaging, release timing, and batch reliability can matter as much as API.

5. Demand stress

Competitor shortage signals and order-fill pressure

Shortages often accelerate because demand spikes hit before the supply chain can adjust.

6. Recovery

Time to switch, release, and return to green

This is the operational resilience question government actually cares about once a disruption begins.

What It Is Not

Not a promise of a live national platform today

  • Not a claim that federal systems are already integrated.
  • Not a claim that a standing mechanism is already authorized.
  • Not a substitute for Health Canada or PHAC systems.
  • Not proof of current all-molecule production readiness.
What It Is

A concrete transparency and assurance appendix

  • An illustrative model for selected oral CVDL medicines.
  • A way to show earlier warning, better bottleneck visibility, and clearer recovery logic.
  • A practical answer to “what reporting and transparency would this supplier actually provide?”
  • A tighter companion to the federal deck, not a separate grand pitch.
