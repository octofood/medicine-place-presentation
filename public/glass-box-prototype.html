Purpose for Health Canada
A shared transparency view for shortage prevention, response, and confidential supply-chain coordination.

This prototype shows how Medicine Place could give Health Canada a consistent view of medicines at risk, the source of each signal, the response underway, and the information that would remain confidential or aggregated. It is designed to complement Drug Shortages Canada and federal shortage-management processes, not replace them.

Illustrative data Regulator view Snapshot: 2026-04-05

What Health Canada can see

Early shortage signals, supplier concentration, stock coverage, impacted products, current response actions, and the confidence level behind each alert.

Decisions it supports

Whether a risk needs monitoring, partner coordination, additional information from the company, or preparation for formal reporting and mitigation discussions.

Boundary of this prototype

No live feeds are connected. Dates, counts, suppliers, and thresholds are representative examples used to demonstrate the transparency model.

What This Is

What it is not

  • Not a live system connected to production data feeds
  • Not a claim to any official or regulatory status
  • Not a replacement for Drug Shortages Canada or any federal system
  • Not evidence that every feed is already operating at scale today

What it is

  • A concrete example of how shortage-risk signals could be shared earlier
  • A model for linking public, confidential, and internal supply-chain data
  • A regulator-facing view of source, cadence, confidence, and response status
  • A practical implementation path for a secure transparency dashboard
Governance & Confidentiality

The dashboard separates what can be shared openly from what should be shared only through a secure regulator channel. This supports Health Canada's information-sharing goals while respecting commercially sensitive supply-chain details.

Shared with Health Canada

Risk status, impacted medicines, source category, last updated date, response owner, expected mitigation timing, and summary supply position.

Aggregated or masked

Supplier names, facility locations, market prices, customer-level distribution, and batch-level inventory can be summarized unless detail is required for mitigation.

Public communication layer

Patient- and provider-facing updates would use approved shortage status, expected availability, alternatives, and mitigation steps without exposing sensitive commercial data.

How This Matches Health Canada's Plan (2024–2028)

Health Canada's Building Resilience (2024–2028) plan emphasizes planning, prevention, response, enhanced reporting, wider data sources, confidential information sharing, and clear public communication. This crosswalk shows where the prototype supports those priorities.
